What is cosplay? From comics to video games.

With the popularity of the CBS show The Big Bang Theory highlighting the lives of nerds and mentioning ComicCon and Cosplay. It is no wonder that may more are discovering the community. Cosplay is a term originated by the Japanese, literally meaning costume play. This is a form of art, where those who participate put together costumes of their favorite characters from japanese anime, manga and video games. Since then the world of cosplay has grown enormously. From simple shirt and pants combo to full body armor made from thermoplastics and craftfoam, all of the members of this nerdy community all are just finding others similar interests. Some cosplayers can even make a living from their cosplay costumes and even some become professional in prop making.So what can begin as a fun crafty hobby could eventually become a way of living for many. Not only do the cosplayers have personal connections many connetions made with the people who run the conventions to professional photographers are made on the ever growing social media sites.

The rapid growth See for yourself.

Simply put, from early 2000 to present time cosplay has dirastically changed. Not only has the ammount of attendees grown but so have the costumes. Now people are able to make full body armor and props that could be on par with movie production level, from professional looking paintign to custom LED lights to motion detecting sounds. Aside from the costume and props the makeup for cosplay has also changed, before it was acceptable for just basic eyeliner, now cosplayers are adapting to more professional level makeup specified for all day wear and close up photos.

What good does it do? For the economy and technology

While currently facebook is the most widely used in the U.S, there are many more social media sites to feature one's work, such as; Worldcosplay , Cosplay.com, and Cure (the most popular). Not only does the increasing popularity in cosplay promote the use of social media as a form of managing a personal business, of seling photos and other handmade items, it also greatly impacts the loacl economy where the conventions are held. An article from the Tampa Bay Business Journal states that the convention Metrocon in 2014 pulled in over 10,000 people to the convention center. It is more than just a gathering of people but an opportunity for the city to get an increase in revenue with all the people traveling buying food and staying in hotels.
